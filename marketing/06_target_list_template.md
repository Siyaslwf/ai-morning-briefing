# Cold Outreach — Target List

Goal: 20 high-quality Latvian/Baltic prospects in 2 hours.

## Where to find them (all free)

### Real Estate (target 5)
- [Latio.lv](https://www.latio.lv) — find agency names from listings
- [City24.lv](https://www.city24.lv) — same approach
- LinkedIn search: `"real estate" Riga` + filter "Company"

### Law Firms (target 5)
- [Legal500 Latvia rankings](https://www.legal500.com/c/latvia/) — top-tier list
- [Chambers Europe Latvia](https://chambers.com/legal-rankings/latvia-100000019)
- LinkedIn: `"law firm" Latvia`

### Marketing Agencies (target 4)
- [Clutch.co Latvia](https://clutch.co/lv/agencies/digital) — sorted by rating
- LinkedIn: `"marketing agency" Riga`

### E-commerce / Retail (target 3)
- Search [Pasts.lv](https://pasts.lv) or [Omniva.lv](https://omniva.lv) for active sellers
- LinkedIn: `"e-commerce" OR "online store" Latvia`

### Recruitment (target 3)
- LinkedIn search: `"recruitment" Riga`
- [WorkInEstonia](https://www.workinestonia.com) — recruiters listed
- Look for any agency posting active tech roles on LinkedIn

## Finding email addresses (legally + free)

### Method 1 — Company website
Most have `info@company.lv` or `kontakts@company.lv` listed in the footer.
Pro tip: real estate agencies and law firms publish individual agent/lawyer emails.

### Method 2 — LinkedIn → email pattern
1. Get the person's name on LinkedIn
2. Get the company's email domain from their website
3. Try patterns: `first.last@`, `first@`, `firstinitial.last@`
4. Verify with [Hunter.io email verifier](https://hunter.io/email-verifier) — free 25/month

### Method 3 — Apollo.io (free 50 credits/month)
- Sign up at [apollo.io](https://apollo.io)
- Search by company → get verified email
- Best for medium-sized companies

## Personalization research (90 seconds per prospect)

Before sending, spend 90 seconds on each prospect:
1. Open their company website → About page
2. Read their most recent blog post or news entry
3. Find ONE specific thing to reference in the email's opening line

This is the #1 thing that distinguishes a reply-getting email from spam.

**Examples of good personalization:**
- "Saw your recent post about [topic]…"
- "Noticed [Company Name] just opened a new office in Tallinn…"
- "Read your case study on [client]…"

**Bad personalization (worse than none):**
- "Hi [First Name], I love what you do" ← obviously a template
- "[Company Name] is doing great work" ← vague
- "Saw your website" ← lazy

---

## When to stop

If after 30 emails (10 per industry × 3 industries):
- 0 replies → your offer is unclear. Rewrite the value prop.
- 1-2 replies → keep going, you're on track.
- 3+ replies → you have product-market fit. Send 30 more in week 2.

If after 60 emails and 0 paying clients → pause outreach. The issue isn't
emails, it's positioning. Talk to whoever DID reply (even "not interested")
and ask: "What would have made you say yes?"
